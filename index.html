Comparative Study: Bible & Quran

Welcome!

This website aims to present passages from the Holy Bible and the Holy Quran side-by-side for comparative study and reflection. The goal is to foster understanding and allow readers to explore the rich textual traditions of these faiths.

Sources: For Biblical passages, references are generally in line with versions like the KJV or NIV, often cross-referenced with tools like the Blue Letter Bible for textual details. For Quranic passages, references are typically from well-regarded English translations, with conceptual links to resources like Corpus Quran for linguistic analysis.

Note: This site presents texts for personal study. It does not aim to provide definitive theological interpretations or declare one text "correct" over another. Such matters are deeply personal and best explored through individual faith, study, and guidance from qualified religious scholars.

Fun Facts & Reflections Corner

Did You Know?

Linguistic Connections: Many names of prophets are similar in Hebrew, Aramaic, and Arabic, reflecting shared Semitic roots. For example, Abraham (Hebrew) is Ibrahim (Arabic).

Calligraphy: Both Christian and Islamic traditions have rich histories of beautiful calligraphy for their sacred texts. Explore examples of illuminated manuscripts and Quranic calligraphy!
